# Life Insurance Underwriting Guidelines
*Sterling Insurance Company - 2025 Edition*

## Overview
These guidelines establish the comprehensive framework for life insurance underwriting decisions, incorporating automated AI analysis capabilities while maintaining industry-standard risk assessment principles.

- **Maximum Coverage**: $10M per applicant
- **Age Limits**: 18-75 years at application

- **Medical Exam Required**: Coverage ≥ $500K
- **Sample Premium**: $32.50/month (Male 30, $500K, 20-year term)

### **Whole Life Insurance**
- **Maximum Coverage**: $15M per applicant
- **Age Limits**: 0-85 years at application
- **Medical Exam Required**: Coverage ≥ $250K
- **Cash Value**: Guaranteed growth with dividends
- **Sample Premium**: $245/month (Male 30, $500K permanent)

### **Universal Life Insurance**
- **Maximum Coverage**: $15M per applicant
- **Age Limits**: 0-85 years at application
- **Flexible Premiums**: Variable payment options
- **Investment Options**: Multiple portfolio choices
- **Sample Premium**: $185/month (Male 30, $500K with standard growth)

## **📊 Risk Assessment Framework**

### **Age-Based Risk Categories**
```
Age 18-35:  Low risk multiplier (0.8-1.0)
Age 36-50:  Standard risk multiplier (1.0-1.3)
Age 51-65:  Moderate risk multiplier (1.3-1.8)
Age 66-75:  High risk multiplier (1.8-2.5)
Age 76-85:  Maximum risk multiplier (2.5-4.0)
```

### **Occupation Classes**
- **Class A (Professional)**: Doctors, lawyers, executives - 0.9x multiplier
- **Class B (Management)**: Business managers, engineers - 1.0x multiplier
- **Class C (Skilled)**: Skilled trades, teachers - 1.1x multiplier
- **Class D (Service)**: Sales, retail workers - 1.2x multiplier
- **Class E (High-Risk)**: Construction, manufacturing - 1.5x multiplier
- **Class F (Aviation/Hazardous)**: Pilots, miners, offshore workers - 2.0x multiplier

### **Health Risk Factors**
#### **Preferred Plus (0.8x premium)**
- Excellent health with no chronic conditions
- BMI 19-26, non-smoker
- No family history of major diseases
- Regular exercise and healthy lifestyle

#### **Preferred (1.0x premium)**
- Good health with minor, controlled conditions
- BMI 27-29, non-smoker
- Limited family history concerns
- Generally healthy lifestyle

#### **Standard Plus (1.3x premium)**
- Controlled chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension)
- BMI 30-34, former smoker (2+ years)
- Moderate family history concerns
- Some lifestyle risk factors

#### **Standard (1.6x premium)**
- Multiple controlled conditions
- BMI 35-39, recent former smoker (1-2 years)
- Significant family history
- Several lifestyle concerns

#### **Substandard (2.0-4.0x premium)**
- Serious medical conditions requiring individual assessment
- BMI ≥40, current smoker
- Extensive adverse medical/family history
- High-risk lifestyle factors

## **🏥 Medical Underwriting**

### **Medical Exam Requirements**
#### **No Medical Exam Required**
- Coverage < $250K (Whole Life)
- Coverage < $500K (Term Life)
- Ages 18-50 with standard health questionnaire
- No adverse medical history

#### **Basic Medical Exam**
- Coverage $250K - $1M
- Nurse paramedical exam
- Basic blood and urine tests
- Height, weight, blood pressure
- EKG if age ≥ 45

#### **Comprehensive Medical Exam**
- Coverage $1M - $5M
- Physician examination
- Comprehensive blood panel
- EKG and stress test
- Chest X-ray if indicated

#### **Extended Medical Exam**
- Coverage $5M+
- Specialist consultations if needed
- Advanced cardiac testing
- Additional screening based on age/history
- Cognitive assessment if age ≥ 70

### **Common Medical Conditions - Risk Ratings**
#### **Cardiovascular Conditions**
- **Well-controlled hypertension**: Standard Plus (+30%)
- **Controlled diabetes (Type 2)**: Standard to Substandard (+60-200%)
- **Previous heart attack**: Individual assessment (typically +200-400%)
- **High cholesterol (controlled)**: Preferred to Standard (+0-60%)

#### **Respiratory Conditions**
- **Asthma (well-controlled)**: Preferred to Standard Plus (+0-30%)
- **Sleep apnea (treated)**: Standard (+60%)
- **Previous lung issues**: Individual assessment

#### **Other Common Conditions**
- **Depression (stable, treated)**: Standard (+60%)
- **Cancer (5+ years remission)**: Standard Plus to Substandard (+30-200%)
- **Kidney stones**: Preferred to Standard (+0-60%)
- **Back problems**: Standard to Substandard based on severity

## **⚡ Automated Decision Rules**

### **Instant Approval Criteria**
All conditions must be met:
- Ages 18-50
- Coverage ≤ 15x annual income
- Non-smoker, BMI 19-29
- No adverse medical history
- Standard occupations (Classes A-D)
- Clean motor vehicle record
- No hazardous activities

### **Conditional Approval**
Requires additional review but likely approval:
- Ages 51-65 with minor health issues
- Coverage 15-20x income with justification
- Controlled chronic conditions
- Occupation Classes E-F with safety protocols
- Former smoker (2+ years)

### **Manual Review Required**
- Ages 66+ (all applications)
- Coverage >20x income
- Current smoker or significant health issues
- High-risk occupations without safety measures
- Recent major medical events
- Financial inconsistencies
- Legal or regulatory concerns

## ** International Considerations**

### **Foreign Travel & Residence**
- **Low-risk countries**: No additional premium
- **Moderate-risk countries**: +25-50% premium
- **High-risk countries**: +100% premium or exclusions
- **Conflict areas**: Coverage suspended or declined
- **Frequent business travel**: Individual assessment based on destinations

### **Foreign Nationals**
- **US residents with permanent status**: Standard underwriting
- **Temporary residents**: Additional restrictions may apply
- **Income verification** more extensive
- **Medical records** require translation and verification

## **🔄 Appeals & Reconsideration**

### **Appeal Triggers**
- Declined applications with marginal risk factors
- Substandard ratings with improved health status
- Premium quotes significantly above expectations
- New medical information or test results
- Changed circumstances (occupation, lifestyle)

### **Reconsideration Process**
1. **Formal appeal** submission with supporting documentation
2. **Senior underwriter** review with fresh perspective
3. **Medical director** consultation if health-related
4. **Updated risk assessment** with new information
5. **Final decision** with detailed explanation
