# Risk Assessment Matrix
*Sterling Insurance Company - 2025 Edition*

## Overview
This comprehensive risk assessment matrix provides the quantitative framework for all underwriting decisions, enabling consistent evaluation across all product lines and supporting automated AI-driven risk scoring systems.

---

## **🎯 Master Risk Scoring Framework**

### **Overall Risk Score Calculation**
```
Final Risk Score = (Age Factor × Age Weight) + 
                  (Occupation Factor × Occupation Weight) + 
                  (Health Factor × Health Weight) + 
                  (Financial Factor × Financial Weight) + 
                  (Lifestyle Factor × Lifestyle Weight)

Weight Distribution:
- Age: 20%
- Occupation: 25% 
- Health: 30%
- Financial: 15%
- Lifestyle: 10%
```

### **Risk Score to Premium Classification**
```
Risk Score 0.80-0.95: Preferred Plus (-20% premium)
Risk Score 0.96-1.15: Preferred (Standard premium)
Risk Score 1.16-1.40: Standard Plus (+30% premium)
Risk Score 1.41-1.80: Standard (+60% premium)
Risk Score 1.81-3.00: Substandard (+100-300% premium)
Risk Score 3.01+:     Declined or Individual Assessment
```

## **📊 Age Risk Assessment**

### **Life Insurance Age Factors**
```
Age 18-25: 0.85 (Low mortality risk, high policy lapse potential)
Age 26-35: 0.90 (Optimal risk profile, stable life circumstances)
Age 36-45: 1.00 (Standard baseline risk category)
Age 46-55: 1.25 (Increased health screening needs)
Age 56-65: 1.60 (Higher mortality risk, comprehensive review)
Age 66-75: 2.20 (Significant mortality risk, limited coverage)
Age 76-85: 3.50 (Maximum age limits, extensive underwriting)
```

### **Disability Insurance Age Factors**
```
Age 18-25: 1.10 (Higher claim frequency, lifestyle risks)
Age 26-35: 0.95 (Optimal working age, established careers)
Age 36-45: 1.00 (Standard baseline for disability risk)
Age 46-55: 1.15 (Increased musculoskeletal issues)
Age 56-64: 1.35 (Pre-retirement health concerns)
Age 65+:   Not eligible for new LTD coverage
```

### **Age-Specific Considerations**
- **Under 30**: Higher lapse rates, affordability concerns, lifestyle instability
- **30-50**: Prime underwriting years, stable income, family responsibilities
- **Over 50**: Increased medical screening, retirement planning considerations
- **Over 65**: Limited product availability, extensive health evaluation required

## **🏢 Occupation Risk Classification**

### **Detailed Occupation Class Matrix**

#### **Class A - Professional (0.90 multiplier)**
**Mortality Risk**: Lowest
**Disability Risk**: Very Low
**Typical Examples**:
- Physicians (non-surgical specialties)
- Certified Public Accountants
- Software Engineers/Developers
- University Professors
- Pharmacists
- Dentists (general practice)

**Risk Characteristics**:
- Office-based work environment
- High income stability
- Advanced education requirements
- Low physical injury risk
- Generally excellent healthcare access

#### **Class B - Management (1.00 multiplier)**
**Mortality Risk**: Low
**Disability Risk**: Low
**Typical Examples**:
- Corporate Executives
- Business Managers
- Engineers (consulting)
- Attorneys
- Financial Advisors
- Insurance Agents

**Risk Characteristics**:
- Mix of office and client interaction
- Stable income with growth potential
- Moderate stress levels
- Professional qualifications required
- Good healthcare benefits typically

#### **Class C - Skilled (1.10 multiplier)**
**Mortality Risk**: Moderate
**Disability Risk**: Moderate
**Typical Examples**:
- Registered Nurses
- Physical Therapists
- Skilled Technicians
- Computer Specialists
- Electricians (commercial)
- Plumbers (commercial)

**Risk Characteristics**:
- Technical skill requirements
- Some physical demands
- Variable work environments
- Moderate injury potential
- Generally stable employment

#### **Class D - Service (1.25 multiplier)**
**Mortality Risk**: Moderate-High
**Disability Risk**: Moderate-High
**Typical Examples**:
- Sales Representatives (travel-heavy)
- Truck Drivers (regional)
- Restaurant Managers
- Retail Supervisors
- Property Managers  
- Auto Mechanics

**Risk Characteristics**:
- Higher physical demands
- Customer interaction stress
- Variable income potential
- Moderate to high injury risk
- Equipment operation requirements

#### **Class E - High Physical Demand (1.50 multiplier)**
**Mortality Risk**: High
**Disability Risk**: High
**Typical Examples**:
- Construction Workers
- Factory/Manufacturing Workers
- Warehouse Personnel
- Long-haul Truck Drivers
- Landscaping Contractors
- Food Service Workers

**Risk Characteristics**:
- Heavy physical labor requirements
- Exposure to hazardous conditions
- Higher injury and illness rates
- Variable work schedules
- Income volatility

#### **Class F - Hazardous (2.00-4.00 multiplier)**
**Mortality Risk**: Very High
**Disability Risk**: Very High
**Typical Examples**:
- Commercial Airline Pilots
- Offshore Oil Workers
- Underground Miners
- High-rise Construction
- Professional Athletes
- Hazardous Materials Handlers

**Risk Characteristics**:
- Extreme physical demands or high-risk environments
- Specialized skills and extensive training required
- Unpredictable income patterns
- High probability of career-ending injuries
- Often requires individual assessment

## **🔄 Risk Combination Rules**

### **Multiple Risk Factor Integration**
When multiple risk factors are present, the combination rules apply:

#### **Additive Factors** (Cumulative Impact)
- Age + Occupation + Basic Health
- Multiple minor health conditions
- Lifestyle factors (BMI + smoking + alcohol)

#### **Multiplicative Factors** (Compound Impact)
- Major health conditions + high-risk occupation
- Financial irregularities + lifestyle risks
- Multiple high-risk activities

#### **Dominant Factor Rule**
When one risk factor significantly exceeds others:
- Single severe health condition may override other favorable factors
- Extremely hazardous occupation may dominate risk assessment
- Financial irregularities may require individual assessment regardless of other factors

### **Risk Mitigation Credits**
```
Excellent Health Management:   -5% to -10%
Safety Training/Certifications: -5% to -15%
Stable Financial History:      -3% to -8%
Long-term Customer:           -5% to -12%
Multiple Product Bundling:     -8% to -15%
```

## **📊 Quality Assurance & Validation**

### **Risk Score Validation Process**
1. **Historical Claims Analysis**: Correlation between risk scores and actual claims
2. **Peer Comparison**: Benchmarking against industry standards
3. **Regulatory Compliance**: Adherence to state insurance regulations
4. **Bias Testing**: Ensuring fair treatment across demographic groups
5. **Performance Monitoring**: Continuous refinement of risk factors

### **Exception Handling**
- **Medical Director Override**: Complex health conditions
- **Senior Underwriter Review**: Unusual risk combinations
- **Management Approval**: High-value cases or policy exceptions
- **Regulatory Consultation**: Compliance questions or novel situations
- **Actuarial Review**: Risk factor adjustments and pricing validation
